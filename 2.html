PAN 2430 Hybrid Rice Seed

Scientific Name:Oryza sativa

Type:

Hybrid

Description:

PAN 2430 is a high-yield hybrid rice variety developed for diverse growing conditions. Known for its robust performance and adaptability, this hybrid rice offers several key attributes that make it an excellent choice for both small and large-scale farmers.

High Yield:

PAN 2430 is engineered to achieve higher yields compared to traditional varieties, making it economically viable for farmers seeking to maximize production.

Disease Resistance:

This variety is bred to resist common rice diseases such as bacterial blight and blast, reducing the need for chemical interventions and promoting healthier crops.

Enhanced Flavor:

PAN 2430 hybrid rice is celebrated for its excellent flavor, which balances subtle sweetness and a pleasant aroma. This makes it a versatile choice for various culinary applications.

Nutritional Value:

PAN 2430 hybrid rice is not only a delicious staple food but also a nutritious option that contributes to a well-balanced diet. Its combination of carbohydrates, protein, vitamins, and minerals makes it a valuable choice for individuals and families seeking to maintain good health.

1. Soil Preparation and Health:

Choose the Right Location:Select a site with well-draining soil and full sun exposure (6-8 hours daily).

Avoid low-lying areas prone to waterlogging.

Soil Testing:Conduct a soil test to assess pH, nutrient levels (N, P, K), and organic matter content.

Ideal pH for rice cultivation is typically between 5.5 and 7.0.

Organic Matter:Incorporate compost, well-rotted manure, or green manure to improve soil fertility and structure. This enhances nutrient availability and promotes beneficial microbial activity.

2. Crop Rotation:

Rotate Crops:Practice crop rotation with non-rice crops to reduce disease buildup and improve soil health. This helps maintain nutrient balance and soil fertility.

3. Proper Planting Techniques:

Planting Depth:Plant seeds at a depth of about 2-4 cm (0.8-1.6 inches). This depth helps protect the seeds from pests while ensuring adequate moisture for germination.

Spacing:Maintain proper spacing between rows and plants. A typical spacing is about 20-25 cm (8-10 inches) between rows and 15-20 cm (6-8 inches) between plants within a row. Proper spacing allows for good air circulation and light penetration.

4. Water Management:

Consistent Watering: Water regularly to keep soil evenly moist, especially during development. Avoid overwatering to prevent root rot.

Drip Irrigation: Although less common for rice, drip irrigation can be used in certain systems to deliver water directly to the root zone, reducing water wastage and minimizing leaf wetness.

5. Seedling Production

Starting Seeds:Use high-quality, certified seeds to ensure good germination rates and disease resistance.

Light and Temperature:Ensure seedlings receive adequate sunlight (about 6-8 hours daily) or use grow lights if growing indoors. Maintain a warm environment (70-75°F or 21-24°C) for optimal seedling growth.

6. Fertilization

Initial Fertilization:Based on soil test results, select a balanced fertilizer that provides essential nutrients. A common formulation is NPK 10-10-10, but this can vary based on specific soil needs.

Side-Dressing: ide dressing is an important practice in rice cultivation that involves applying additional fertilizers to support plant growth during critical development stages. Choose a nitrogen-rich fertilizer for side dressing, such as urea or ammonium sulfate. This helps support vegetative growth and grain filling. Ensure the selected fertilizer aligns with soil test recommendations and overall nutrient management goals.
