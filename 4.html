RVSM 1135 Hybrid Soyabean

Scientific Name:Glycine max

Type:

Hybrid

Description:

RVSM 1135 is a high-yielding hybrid soybean variety developed for various growing conditions. Known for its adaptability, it performs well in different soil types and climatic conditions. This variety is characterized by its robust plant structure, disease resistance, and strong performance in both dryland and irrigated systems.

High Yield:

RVSM 1135 is bred from high-yielding parent lines, ensuring robust genetic traits that enhance overall productivity.

Disease Resistance:

Bred to withstand common soybean diseases such as soybean cyst nematode (SCN), Phytophthora root rot, and downy mildew, which helps maintain healthy plants and high yields.

Enhanced Flavor:

While soybeans are primarily grown for their oil and protein content, RVSM 1135 offers a pleasant nutty flavor, making it suitable for both culinary uses and food processing.

Nutritional Value:

RVSM 1135 soybeans are rich in protein (around 36-40%) and contain essential fatty acids, vitamins, and minerals.They are also a good source of dietary fiber and contain antioxidants, which contribute to overall health and well-being.

1. Soil Preparation and Health:

Choose the Right Location:Choose well-drained, loamy soils rich in organic matter for optimal growth.

Soil Testing:Conduct soil tests to determine pH and nutrient levels. Ideal soil pH for soybeans is between 6.0 and 7.0.

Organic Matter:Add organic matter such as compost, well-rotted manure, or cover crops to improve soil structure, fertility, and moisture retention. Organic matter enhances microbial activity, which is crucial for healthy soil.

2. Crop Rotation:

Rotate Crops:Follow soybeans with corn in the next growing season, as corn benefits from the nitrogen fixed by soybeans.

3. Proper Planting Techniques:

Planting Depth:Sow seeds at a depth of 1 to 1.5 inches to encourage strong germination.

Spacing:Maintain proper spacing between rows (30 inches) and between plants (3-4 inches) to ensure adequate light and airflow.

4. Water Management:

Consistent Watering: Water regularly to keep soil evenly moist, especially during development. Avoid overwatering to prevent root rot.

Drip Irrigation: Although less common for rice, drip irrigation can be used in certain systems to deliver water directly to the root zone, reducing water wastage and minimizing leaf wetness.

5. Seedling Production

Starting Seeds:Start seeds directly in the field after the last frost date when soil temperatures are consistently above 55°F.

Light and Temperature:Ensure seedlings receive adequate sunlight (about 6-8 hours daily) or use grow lights if growing indoors. Maintain a warm environment (70-75°F or 21-24°C) for optimal seedling growth.

6. Fertilization

Initial Fertilization:Apply a balanced fertilizer at planting, focusing on phosphorus to promote strong root development.

Side-Dressing: Consider side dressing with nitrogen if soil tests indicate low nitrogen levels, particularly during the flowering stage.
