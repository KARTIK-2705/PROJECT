SARPAN HYBRID CUCUMBER - 30 SEEDS

Scientific Name:Cucumis sativus

Type:

Hybrid

Description:

SARPAN Hybrid Cucumber is a high-quality variety known for its crisp texture, smooth skin, and excellent flavor. Ideal for both fresh consumption and pickling, this hybrid offers high yields and disease resistance.

High Yield:

SARPAN cucumbers produce a large number of fruits per plant, making them suitable for both home gardens and commercial production.

Disease Resistance:

Enhanced Flavor:

Known for its refreshing taste, SARPAN cucumbers have a slightly sweet flavor profile, making them perfect for salads, sandwiches, and snacks.

1. Soil Preparation and Health:

Choose the Right Location:Choose a site that receives full sun for at least 6-8 hours a day, which is crucial for optimal growth and fruit production.

Soil Testing:Prefers well-drained, loamy soil rich in organic matter. Soil pH should ideally be between 6.0 and 6.8.Conduct soil tests to determine nutrient levels and amend accordingly to create optimal growing conditions.

Organic Matter:Add organic matter such as compost, well-rotted manure, or cover crops to improve soil structure, fertility, and moisture retention. Organic matter enhances microbial activity, which is crucial for healthy soil.

2. Crop Rotation:

Rotate Crops:Rotate cucumbers with non-cucurbit crops (such as legumes or cereals) to help reduce disease and pest buildup in the soil.

3. Proper Planting Techniques:

Planting Depth:Sow seeds at a depth of about 1 inch to ensure good germination.

Spacing:Space plants 12-18 inches apart in rows that are 3-4 feet apart to allow for ample growth and air circulation.

4. Water Management:

Consistent Watering: Consistent watering is crucial, especially during flowering and fruit development. Aim to keep the soil consistently moist but not waterlogged..

Drip Irrigation: Consider using drip irrigation to minimize water on the leaves, reducing the risk of fungal diseases.

5. Seedling Production

Starting Seeds:Start seeds indoors 3-4 weeks before the last frost or sow directly in the garden after frost risk has passed.

Light and Temperature:Optimal soil temperature for germination is between 70°F and 95°F.

6. Fertilization

Initial Fertilization:Apply a balanced fertilizer at planting time to promote strong root development.

Side-Dressing: Side dress with additional nitrogen fertilizer when plants begin to vine to support growth and fruit development.
