1. Soil Preparation and Health:

Choose the Right Location:Select a site with well-draining soil and full sun exposure (6-8 hours daily).

Soil Testing:Test the soil for pH and nutrient levels. Amend as necessary to create optimal growing conditions.

Organic Matter:Incorporate compost or well-rotted manure to improve soil fertility and structure.

2. Crop Rotation:

Rotate Crops:Avoid planting tomatoes (and other nightshades) in the same location year after year. Rotate with non-nightshade crops to reduce disease buildup in the soil.

3. Proper Planting Techniques:

Planting Depth:Plant seedlings deeply to encourage strong root systems.

Spacing:Ensure adequate spacing between plants to improve air circulation and reduce humidity around the leaves, helping to prevent fungal diseases.

4. Water Management:

Consistent Watering: Water regularly to keep soil evenly moist, especially during fruit development. Avoid overwatering to prevent root rot.

Drip Irrigation: Consider using drip irrigation to deliver water directly to the roots, minimizing leaf wetness and reducing disease risk.

5. Seedling Production

Starting Seeds:Start seeds indoors 6-8 weeks before the last expected frost date. Use seed trays or pots filled with seed-starting mix.

Light and Temperature:Keep seedlings in a warm location (70-75Â°F) with plenty of light, using grow lights if necessary.

6. Fertilization

Initial Fertilization:Apply a balanced fertilizer (e.g., 10-10-10) at planting time.

Side-Dressing: Fertilize again mid-season with a nitrogen-rich fertilizer as the plants begin to set fruit to support growth.
